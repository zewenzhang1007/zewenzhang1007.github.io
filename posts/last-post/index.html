Final Project

By Zewen Zhang

November 20, 2020

In this post, I’d like to introduce my final project: Covid-19 in US-case and individual level analysis.

At the beginning of the project, we start with preparing the data. There are four major part of the data our projects: policy data, hospital data, individual data as well as population data.

The data I collected is population data from United States Census Bureau, American Community Survey 1-Year Data (2005-2019). This dataset includes state level population data, describing the number of cases of female/male under each group for each state. The column names od original data is only code representing location of the dataset, I transformed them into meaningful index manually:

To present our project in a straight forward way, we decided to build an app with streamlit. There are four parts:

  1. Overview of all data, including new cases and new death. Users can select the date to look at, as well as looking at the map plot to see the data of each state.

  2. State level comparison, focusing on policy comparison on state level, because we want to know if the policies about Covid-19 would or would not have an impact on the Covid-19 data. Users can choose date, the range of day for analysis, as well as which policy to see the outcome: ‘5 states that “stay at home order” performs best’ & ‘5 states that “stay at home order” performs worst’, and ‘Relative Increase Rate in map’

  3. Case level prediction, we used random forest to build a predictive model, users can choose to look at overview level (the whole country) or state level. Users can choose the maximum number of features they want to put in the data, and the model will list out the most important features while predicting.

  4. Individual level analysis, the part I was responsible for. The data I used is the individual cases data, which was collected by my teammate zhuoran. However, the original dataset is too large (over 2,000,000), and it is not able to be run in streamlit app, therefore I have to choose part of it. The dataset is list as the rank of age (starts from people in 0-9 years, end with people over 80 years), thus I decided to take 5 percent of original data for each age group. The tree plots of original data and the sampled data look very similar.

After preparing the data, I plot the count of death/infection cases, as well as the death/infection rate, users can choose to look at from race level or age group level. Also I compared the difference between male and female for these two parts.

In the end, I’d like to say that our project is very complete and detailed, and I want to thank all my teammates for this great job.
